# Facial Recognition in Law Enforcement Case Study

## What This Is

This case study looks at facial recognition in law enforcement from both directions: where it can help and where it can do damage. In that setting, accuracy metrics alone are not enough. A system can appear effective on paper and still create serious problems when bias, privacy, due process, and public trust enter the picture.

## What I Took From It

- high-impact AI systems have to be judged by consequences, not just performance claims
- bias in identification systems is not a side issue when real people absorb the error
- public-sector deployment raises a different level of scrutiny than commercial convenience features

## Concepts In Play

- computer vision context
- fairness and bias analysis
- privacy risk
- public trust
- ethical evaluation of AI systems

## Why I Included It

I wanted this in the portfolio because it shows I do not treat AI as automatically good because it is technically possible. Some systems need harder questions, not faster adoption.
